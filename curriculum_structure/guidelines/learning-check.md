# Daily Learning Check (DLC)

## What is DLC?

DLC stands for Daily Learning Check. It is an evaluative and feedback tool used at the end of class sessions to assess students' understanding of the day's topic and to gather their feedback on the session. This tool typically takes the form of a digital questionnaire that includes multiple-choice quiz questions related to the session's content and feedback-related questions.

## Objective of DLC

The primary objectives of the DLC are:

1. To evaluate students' comprehension of the material presented in the day's class.
2. To provide a means for students to reflect on their learning experience.
3. To gather actionable feedback for instructors or instructional assistants (IAs) on the effectiveness of their teaching methods and content delivery.
4. To identify areas that may require additional clarification or emphasis in future sessions.
5. To ensure that the course remains student-centered and responsive to the learners' needs.

## Process

The process of the DLC can be summarized as follows:

1. **Distribution**: At the end of the class, the instructor or IA releases the DLC form.
2. **Completion**: Students are given approximately 10 minutes to complete the quiz and provide their feedback.
3. **Submission**: Once students have completed the DLC, they submit their responses and leave the session.
4. **Analysis**: The responses from the DLC are compiled into a DLC Report.
5. **Review**: Instructors and IAs review the DLC Report to analyze session effectiveness, student understanding, and feedback.
6. **Action**: Based on the DLC Report, necessary adjustments are made to the course content, teaching methods, or other aspects as identified from the feedback.

## Template Creation

The template for the DLC should be standardized across different courses to maintain consistency. It should include the following elements:

1. **Identification**: Sections for student code and name to uniquely identify the respondent.
2. **Quiz Questions**: Multiple-choice questions that cover key concepts from the session.
3. **Feedback Questions**: Rating scales for the session and instructor, as well as open-ended questions for detailed feedback.
4. **Additional Comments**: Space for any other comments or suggestions from the students.

Each element should be clearly labeled and easy to navigate, ensuring that students can complete the form quickly and efficiently. The design should be clean and uncluttered to avoid confusion or errors in response.
