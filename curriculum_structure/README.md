# Curriculum Repository Overview

## Introduction

Welcome to our Curriculum Repository, a centralized platform designed to transition our course resources from an unstructured to a structured format. This repository acts as the definitive source for all essential educational materials, including lecture notes, student notes, and beyond.

## Purpose

This initiative is driven by the necessity for a cohesive and standardized approach to managing our curriculum, streamlining access to educational content, and fostering consistency across all courses.

## Problems Addressed

In our previous decentralized system, individual instructors and Instructional Assistants (IAs) maintained their notes in various formats (e.g., Notion, Google Docs), leading to several challenges:

- **Lack of Standardization:** With each instructor maintaining a unique curriculum version, we faced difficulties in maintaining a consistent educational standard and tracking coverage across different classes.
  
- **Difficulty in Identifying Gaps:** When feedback from placements or outcomes teams highlighted conceptual weaknesses in students, pinpointing these gaps became challenging due to the disparate nature of the curriculum materials.
  
- **Inconsistent Synchronization:** Limited visibility into the specifics of what was taught, including subtopics and examples used in lectures or assignments given, created dependencies. IAs often needed to contact instructors directly for this information, complicating the process for leadership or new instructors to understand or access curriculum content.
  
- **Redundancy in Curriculum Development:** The tendency to redesign the curriculum for new courses, even when existing materials could be adapted, led to unnecessary duplication of effort.
  
- **Operational Overhead:** Significant time spent by instructors and IAs on creating and managing lectures, assignments, and other materials detracted from their primary focus on curriculum development.

## Solution: Central Curriculum Repository

Addressing these challenges, the Central Curriculum Repository provides a unified, accessible platform for curriculum management. It allows for collaborative updates and review through Pull Requests (PRs), overseen by a lead reviewer.

## Benefits

The implementation of this repository brings forth multiple advantages:

- **Consistency and Standardization:** Ensures a uniform curriculum framework across all courses, promoting a consistent learning experience.
  
- **Visibility and Accessibility:** Facilitates immediate access to curriculum details for all teams (Curriculum, Outcomes, etc.), eliminating the need for direct inquiries and fostering an environment of transparency.
  
- **Improved Synchronization:** Enhances coordination among course team members, providing clarity on taught content, assigned homework, and overall curriculum flow. This aids IAs in standups and offers the outcomes team clear visibility into curriculum coverage.
  
- **Efficiency:** Reduces the operational workload on educators, allowing more focus on enriching the curriculum rather than on administrative tasks. This centralized approach also prevents the redundancy of redesigning curriculums for new courses, encouraging the reuse of existing, high-quality materials.

## Conclusion

By centralizing our curriculum resources in this repository, we aim to address the core issues identified through direct examples and feedback. This structured approach not only enhances the educational delivery and resource management but also solidifies the foundation of our curriculum, ensuring a more effective and cohesive learning experience for all stakeholders involved.

## Track Template Repository

This is the Track Template Repository where all the guidelines, learning objectives, and materials of different programs and topics exist.

### Guidelines Folder

The `guidelines` folder contains the important pointers to be followed for the particular section like assignment, instructor-notes, etc. Also, a sample structured template is provided to ensure consistency and clarity.

### Learning Objectives

The `learning-objectives` folder contains the list of learning objectives with LO id for the courses of the particular track. It also includes Learning Objectives guidelines which instruct the creation of LOs, ensuring that all educational goals are clearly defined and aligned with course content.

### Programs

The `programs` folder contains all the programs that are running in Masai, such as Masai One named as `ms1`, `cn`, `college`, etc. Under each program, there are courses, which contain a list of sessions. Each session includes session notes, assignments, learning checks, etc., for that particular session.

### Topics

The `topics` folder contains a detailed overview of the LO mentioned in the learning objectives sheet, providing educators and students with a comprehensive understanding of each topic's scope and significance.
